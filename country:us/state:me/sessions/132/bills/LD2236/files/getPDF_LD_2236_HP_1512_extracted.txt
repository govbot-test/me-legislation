1 SUMMARY
22 This bill repeals a provision in law that allows an applicant for a hunting or fishing
33 license to indicate that the applicant's e-mail address is confidential and that requires that
44 information be held confidential by the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife. It
55 creates a new section of law related to records held by the department and databases the
66 department contributes to related to department licenses, permits, registrations and
77 applications. The bill also defines "data," "commercial record," "noncommercial record"
88 and "personally identifiable information" within a noncommercial record and within a
99 commercial record. There is more personally identifiable information held confidential for
1100 a noncommercial record than for a commercial record.
11 The bill provides exemptions to holding personally identifiable information
12 confidential, including exceptions for law enforcement purposes in accordance with the
13 Maine Revised Statutes, Title 16, section 805-A, fish and wildlife management purposes,
14 other state and federal government entities and the person who is the subject of the record
15 or that person's agent.
16 The bill provides that data contained in a records database, whether individual or
17 multiple records, maintained, administered or contributed to by the department that is
18 considered confidential may not be publicly disseminated.
19 The bill provides that the department may publicly disseminate nonpersonally
20 identifiable data that is contained in a records database and may provide copies of
21 individual records and whole or partial databases of records to individuals, businesses and
22 other entities.
23 The bill provides that the department may charge reasonable fees for both individual
24 record requests and database requests at the expense of the person making the request. The
25 cost of furnishing a copy of the record is not subject to the limitations of the Maine Revised
26 Statutes, Title 1, section 408-A. The bill creates all-terrain vehicle and snowmobile
27 accident reporting system exemptions that mirror the Department of Public Safety's
28 exemption for motor vehicle crash report data that is held in the Maine Crash Reporting
29 Online Search and Ordering Service. These provisions allow any nonpersonally identifiable
30 all-terrain vehicle and snowmobile accident report data to be released to the public, and the
31 cost to process these data requests and release the records is not subject to the limitations
32 within the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 1, section 408-A. These provisions also create
33 definitions for "data," "nonpersonally identifiable accident report data" and "personally
34 identifiable accident report data."
35 The bill also provides that all-terrain vehicle and snowmobile accident reports or
36 statements contained within have specific uses for law enforcement purposes and the
37 agency can disseminate individual records or many records within records databases in
38 accordance with this law. All-terrain vehicle and snowmobile registration numbers may be
39 released only under an affidavit restricting the use and misuse of registration numbers, and
40 failure to comply with reporting obligations is a Class E crime.
