17 §2107-A. Automated crime victim notification system; minimum requirements
18 A facility that is required by section 2106 to provide notification to a victim of a
19 defendant's release or escape or that is required by section 2107 to provide notification to
20 a victim of a defendant's release on preconviction bail and that provides such notification
21 using an automated crime victim notification system shall ensure that:
22 1. Optional use of automated system. A victim is not required to receive the
23 notifications under section 2106 or 2107 using the automated crime victim notification
24 system and may elect to receive the notification through other methods specified in section
25 2106 or 2107;
26 2. Disclosure of protected information. The automated crime victim notification
27 system is designed to protect from disclosure the name, contact information and any other
28 personally identifying information about a victim who registers to use the system except
29 disclosure:
30 A. To a sexual assault counselor, as defined in Title 16, section 53-A, subsection 1,
31 paragraph B;
32 B. To a domestic violence advocate, as defined in Title 16, section 53-B, subsection
33 1, paragraph A-4;

18 Sec. 2. Establishment of automated crime victim notification system.
19 County sheriffs, collectively through a statewide association of sheriffs, shall establish an
20 automated crime victim notification system for use in jails, county correctional facilities
21 and regional correctional facilities. The system under this section must be designed to
22 provide victims and other registrants the option of receiving automated notices of
23 information available to victims pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 17-A,
24 sections 2106 and 2107. In developing, maintaining and administering the system under
25 this section, the sheriffs, collectively through a statewide association of sheriffs, may
26 contract with an entity that develops or offers commercial, real-time automated crime
27 victim notification systems.
28 Sec. 3. Automated crime victim notification system advisory committee;
29 report. County sheriffs, collectively through a statewide association of sheriffs, shall
30 convene an automated crime victim notification system advisory committee to support,
31 coordinate and monitor the implementation of the automated crime victim notification
32 system required by section 2. The advisory committee under this section must include
33 representatives from a statewide association of sheriffs, a statewide coalition of domestic
34 violence prevention projects, a statewide coalition of sexual assault support centers and the
35 Office of Victim Services within the Department of Corrections; may invite the
36 participation of other members as appropriate; may consult with other individuals, agencies
37 or organizations as necessary to support its work; shall meet at least monthly; and shall, at
38 a minimum, review and develop findings and recommendations regarding the following
39 matters:
40 1. The resources included or proposed for inclusion in the directory of community-
41 based support services;
42 2. The gaps or challenges experienced by crime victims who may elect to register or
43 are registered to use the automated crime victim notification system required by section 2
44 as that system is implemented and refined; and
