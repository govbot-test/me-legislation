132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
SECOND REGULAR SESSION-2026
Legislative Document No. 2079
An Act to Amend the Laws Governing Municipal Reporting on
Residential Buildings
Approved for introduction by a majority of the Legislative Council pursuant to Joint Rule

Section 2:
203.
Reference to the Committee on Housing and Economic Development suggested and
ordered printed.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative GERE of Kennebunkport.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 5 MRSA §3241, sub-§5, as enacted by PL 2025, c. 495, §2, is amended to
3 read:
4 5. Municipal housing data reporting. By January March 31st of each year, a
5 municipality with 4,000 or more residents shall provide to the office for use in
6 administering the program an annual a report of that includes the following information
7 from the prior calendar year:
8 A. Residential The number of residential units approved through residential building
9 permit applications, including the number of applications submitted, approved and
10 denied, categorized into single-family, single-family with the following residential
11 building types: accessory dwelling unit, 2-to-4-family and more-than-4-family homes
12 single-family housing unit, 2-unit housing, 3-to-4-unit housing and housing with 5 or
13 more units. The number of units that are income-restricted must also be reported if
14 known;
15 B. The number of dwelling units permitted and demolished approved for demolition;
16 and
17 C. The number of residential units approved through certificates of occupancy or other
18 final approvals of housing units issued, the number of those housing units affordable
19 to persons or families whose incomes are 80% or less of the area median income as
20 determined by the Maine State Housing Authority and the number of those housing
21 units affordable to persons or families whose incomes are from 81% to 120% of the
22 area median income as determined by the Maine State Housing Authority categorized
23 into the following residential building types: accessory dwelling unit, single-family
24 housing unit, 2-unit housing, 3-to-4-unit housing and housing with 5 or more units.
25 As used in this subsection, "accessory dwelling unit" has the same meaning as in Title
26 30‑A, section 4301, subsection 1‑C.
27 The office shall encourage municipalities with fewer than 4,000 residents to report the
28 information required by this subsection.
29 SUMMARY
3300 This bill amends the municipal housing information that must be provided to the Maine
3311 Office of Community Affairs by municipalities with 4,000 or more residents. It also
3322 changes the timing of the report and directs the office to encourage municipalities with
3333 fewer than 4,000 residents to report the same information.
