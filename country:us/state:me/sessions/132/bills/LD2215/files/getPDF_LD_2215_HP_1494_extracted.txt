132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
SECOND REGULAR SESSION-2026
Legislative Document No. 2215
An Act Relating to Nonwater-dependent Floating Structures
Reported by Representative ROBERTS of South Berwick for the Joint Standing Committee
on Inland Fisheries and Wildlife pursuant to Public Law 2025, chapter 378, section 13.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 12 MRSA §13056, sub-§3-A, ¶B, as enacted by PL 2025, c. 378, §10, is
3 amended to read:
4 B. Meets all applicable operating visibility requirements under 46 Code of Federal
5 Regulations, Section 144.905. The watercraft must include a position from which its
6 operator is afforded unobstructed forward visibility to each side of the centerline for at
7 least 70 degrees from the operator's position and is afforded unobstructed lateral
8 visibility to each side of the centerline at least 90 degrees from the operator's position
9 standards related to the field of vision from the helm position as recommended by the
10 American Boat and Yacht Council or its successor organization;
11 Sec. 2. 38 MRSA §3301, sub-§1, as enacted by PL 2025, c. 378, §12, is amended
12 to read:
13 1. Abandoned nonwater-dependent floating structure. "Abandoned nonwater-
14 dependent floating structure" means a nonwater-dependent floating structure that has been
15 left in, on or over internal the waters of this State for more than 24 hours without intention
16 of removal by the owner, as determined by the primary enforcement authority.
17 Sec. 3. 38 MRSA §3301, sub-§2, as enacted by PL 2025, c. 378, §12, is repealed.
18 Sec. 4. 38 MRSA §3301, sub-§8, as enacted by PL 2025, c. 378, §12, is repealed.
19 Sec. 5. 38 MRSA §3301, sub-§11, as enacted by PL 2025, c. 378, §12, is amended
20 to read:
21 11. Nonwater-dependent uses. "Nonwater-dependent uses" means those uses that
22 can function in a location other than on the surface waters of the this State and that do not
23 require, for their primary purpose, location on submerged lands or direct access to inland
24 waters or coastal territorial waters.
25 Sec. 6. 38 MRSA §3301, sub-§11-A is enacted to read:
26 11-A. Territorial waters. "Territorial waters" has the same meaning as in Title 12,
27 section 6001, subsection 48-B.
28 Sec. 7. 38 MRSA §3301, sub-§13 is enacted to read:
29 13. Waters of this State. "Waters of this State" has the same meaning as in Title 12,
30 section 13001, subsection 30.
31 Sec. 8. 38 MRSA §3302, first ¶, as enacted by PL 2025, c. 378, §12, is amended
32 to read:
33 Except as otherwise provided in this chapter, a person may not place or use a nonwater-
34 dependent floating structure in, on or over internal the waters of this State.
35 Sec. 9. 38 MRSA §3303, sub-§2, as enacted by PL 2025, c. 378, §12, is amended
36 to read:
37 2. Swimming structures. Any dock, wharf or swimming and diving float that is used
38 as a floating structure for swimming or diving or for the temporary tie-off of watercraft, is
39 placed on internal the waters of the this State and is temporarily or permanently anchored
40 to the bottom of those waters;
Page 1 - 132LR3047(01)
1 Sec. 10. 38 MRSA §3304, sub-§1, as enacted by PL 2025, c. 378, §12, is amended
2 to read:
3 1. Eligibility. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the owner of a
4 nonwater-dependent floating structure that was in existence as of January 1, 2025 may
5 make application to the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, Bureau of
6 Parks and Lands, referred to in this section as "the bureau," through the submerged lands
7 leasing program established pursuant to Title 12, section 1862 for a nonwater-dependent
8 floating structure lease on the internal waters of the this State that authorizes the owner to
9 continue to operate the nonwater-dependent floating structure in accordance with the
10 requirements of this section when the following applies:
11 A. The applicant holds right, title or interest to the shorefront to which the nonwater-
12 dependent floating structure is attached; or
13 B. If the nonwater-dependent floating structure is not attached to the shore, the
14 applicant has right, title or interest to the adjacent upland shorefront and holds an
15 approved mooring from the United States Army Corps of Engineers or the municipality
16 in which the structure is located.
17 Sec. 11. 38 MRSA §3304, sub-§6, ¶B, as enacted by PL 2025, c. 378, §12, is
18 amended to read:
19 B. If the structure is located on coastal territorial waters, it must comply with 33 United
20 States Code, Section 1322.
21 Sec. 12. 38 MRSA §3304, sub-§7, ¶B, as enacted by PL 2025, c. 378, §12, is
22 amended to read:
23 B. Once sited in accordance with a lease issued under this section, a nonwater-
24 dependent floating structure may not be sited in any other location on the internal
25 waters of the this State.
26 Sec. 13. 38 MRSA §3305, sub-§3, as enacted by PL 2025, c. 378, §12, is amended
27 to read:
28 3. Removal of structures. A person who violates section 3302 shall remove the
29 violating nonwater-dependent floating structure from the internal waters of this State that
30 the structure was placed or used in, on or over and is responsible for all costs associated
31 with the removal and the costs of any damage to persons or property caused by the
32 structure's placement or use.
33 SUMMARY
3344 This bill is reported out by the Joint Standing Committee on Inland Fisheries and
3355 Wildlife pursuant to Public Law 2025, chapter 378. The bill makes changes to definitions
3366 related to the laws governing nonwater-dependent floating structures. It replaces the term
3377 "internal waters," which is defined as waters under the exclusive jurisdiction of the State,
3388 with the term "waters of this State," which is defined as all internal waters and all federal
3399 waters within the jurisdiction of the State. It replaces the term "coastal waters" with the
4400 term "territorial waters." The bill also provides that, in order for the owner of a homemade
4411 watercraft that is a houseboat or that is a nonwater-dependent floating structure capable of
4422 being used as a means of transportation on water to obtain a certificate of number from the
Page 2 - 132LR3047(01)
413 Commissioner of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife, the homemade watercraft must meet all
424 applicable operating visibility standards related to the field of vision from the helm position
435 as recommended by the American Boat and Yacht Council or its successor organization.
