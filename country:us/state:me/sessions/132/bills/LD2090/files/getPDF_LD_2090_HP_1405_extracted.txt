132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
SECOND REGULAR SESSION-2026
Legislative Document No. 2090
An Act to Establish a One-year Probationary Period for Public
Safety Dispatchers
Approved for introduction by a majority of the Legislative Council pursuant to Joint Rule

Section 2:
203.
Reference to the Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety suggested and ordered
printed.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative BUNKER of Farmington.
Cosponsored by Representative: LOOKNER of Portland.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 26 MRSA §600-C is enacted to read:
3 §600-C. Probationary period for public safety dispatchers
4 1. Probationary period. The probationary period for a public safety dispatcher may
5 not be less than 12 months.
6 2. Public safety dispatcher. For the purposes of this section, "public safety
7 dispatcher" means a person whose primary employment duties consist of any combination
8 of:
9 A. Acting as an emergency medical dispatcher as defined in Title 32, section 85-A,
10 subsection 1, paragraph D;
11 B. Answering, directing or dispatching the response to public safety requests for
12 service at a public safety answering point as defined in Title 25, section 2921,
13 subsection 7;
14 C. Answering, directing or dispatching the response of emergency services for
15 municipal fire protection pursuant to Title 30-A, chapter 153; or
16 D. Answering, directing or dispatching the response of law enforcement officers as
17 defined in Title 25, section 2801-A, subsection 5.
18 SUMMARY
1199 This bill establishes a one-year probationary period for public safety dispatchers.
