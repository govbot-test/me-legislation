Legislative Document No. 2114
An Act Regarding Public Preschool Programs Serving Children
Who Are 3 Years of Age
Submitted by the Department of Education pursuant to Joint Rule 203.
Reference to the Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs suggested and ordered
printed.

Clerk

1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 20-A MRSA §1, sub-§23-A, as amended by PL 2019, c. 241, §1, is further
3 amended to read:
4 23-A. Public preschool program. "Public preschool program" means a program
5 offered by a public elementary school pursuant to chapter 203 that provides instruction to
6 children who are 3 or 4 years of age, including but not limited to a Head Start program of
7 instruction provided by providers licensed by the Department of Health and Human
8 Services that is approved as a component of the public preschool program.
9 Sec. 2. 20-A MRSA §405, sub-§3, ¶T, as amended by PL 2013, c. 581, §1, is
10 further amended to read:
11 T. Establish and maintain a 5-year plan for education that includes goals and policies
12 for the education of children who are 4 years of age in public preschool programs and
13 children in kindergarten and grades one to 12 and that promotes services for public
14 preschool children. The plan must incorporate and build upon the work of the Task
15 Force on Learning Results established in Public Law 1993, chapter 290 and the federal
16 GOALS 2000: Educate America Act;
17 Sec. 3. 20-A MRSA §4251, first ¶, as amended by PL 2015, c. 267, Pt. C, §1, is
18 further amended to read:
19 The intent of this subchapter is to encourage school administrative units to place an
20 increased emphasis on instruction and curriculum for all children beginning at 4 years of
21 age in public preschool programs to grade 2. This subchapter is not intended as a method
22 of financing existing efforts but as a way of encouraging the development of new or
23 expanded programs.
24 Sec. 4. 20-A MRSA §4252, sub-§4, as amended by PL 2007, c. 141, §3, is further
25 amended to read:
26 4. Programs for children 3 years of age, 4 years of age and 5 years of age.
27 Encourage the development of public preschool programs or 2-year kindergartens in
28 conformity with section 5201, subsection 2, paragraph C, D and E and other appropriate
29 programs to address the needs of children 3 years of age, 4 years of age and 5 years of age;
30 Sec. 5. 20-A MRSA c. 203, sub-c. 3, headnote is amended to read:
31 SUBCHAPTER 3
32 PUBLIC PRESCHOOL PROGRAMS FOR CHILDREN 4 YEARS OF AGE
33 Sec. 6. 20-A MRSA §4271, sub-§2, as enacted by PL 2013, c. 581, §3, is amended
34 to read:
35 2. Allowable costs; children 4 years of age. Beginning with the 2015-2016 school
36 year and for each subsequent school year, the State may provide start-up funding for the
37 allowable costs to operate public preschool programs for children 4 years of age under this
38 subchapter.
39 Sec. 7. 20-A MRSA §4271, sub-§2-A is enacted to read:
Page 1 - 132LR2618(01)
1 2-A. Allowable costs; children 3 years of age with disabilities. Beginning with the
2 2026-2027 school year and for each subsequent school year, in addition to the funding
3 permitted pursuant to subsection 2, the State may provide start-up funding for the allowable
4 costs to operate public preschool programs for children who are 3 years of age and have
5 been identified as a child with a disability pursuant to section 7001, subsection 1-B.
6 Sec. 8. 20-A MRSA §4271, sub-§3, as amended by PL 2017, c. 284, Pt. C, §5, is
7 further amended to read:
8 3. Grant funds. Beginning with the 2015-2016 school year and for each subsequent
9 school year until the 2026-2027 school year, the commissioner may provide start-up
10 funding to qualified school administrative units to operate public preschool programs for
11 children 4 years of age and, beginning with the 2026-2027 school year and for each
12 subsequent school year, public preschool programs for children 3 or 4 years of age. Grants
13 provided for allowable costs for approved public preschool programs must be provided
14 from state, federal or private funds appropriated, allocated or authorized by the Legislature
15 for that purpose.
16 Sec. 9. 20-A MRSA §4271, sub-§4, as enacted by PL 2013, c. 581, §3, is amended
17 to read:
18 4. Qualifications; rules. To qualify for a grant under this section, a school
19 administrative unit must submit an implementation plan to the department for the operation
20 of a new or expanded public preschool program. The qualifications established for
21 implementation plans must contain standards and best practices for public preschool
22 programs and must encourage a school administrative unit to demonstrate coordination
23 with other early childhood programs in the community to maximize resources and provide
24 comprehensive services to meet the needs of children 3 or 4 years of age in accordance
25 with this subchapter and rules adopted by the commissioner. In awarding grants under this
26 section, the commissioner shall give priority to a qualified school administrative unit that
27 has a greater percentage of economically disadvantaged students as determined pursuant to
28 section 15675, subsection 2 than other qualified school administrative units under this
29 subsection and in accordance with the following order of preference:
30 A. The first preference must be to award grant funds to a qualified school
31 administrative unit that does not operate a public preschool program and that submits
32 a plan for the development and operation of a new public preschool program; and
33 B. The 2nd preference must be to award grant funds to a qualified school
34 administrative unit that operates a public preschool program and that submits a plan
35 for the development and operation of an expanded public preschool program.
36 The commissioner shall adopt rules that establish criteria for the approval of
37 implementation plans and for the awarding of start-up funds for the allowable costs of
38 operating public preschool programs. Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine
39 technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2‑A.

41 to read:
42 5. Application for federal public preschool funds. The department may apply for
43 assistance from the Federal Government for the development of public preschool programs
44 for children 3 or 4 years of age on behalf of school administrative units in the State. The

415 department shall administer any federal funds received for the benefit of public preschool
426 programs in the State. As the designated state agency authorized to administer federal
437 funds, the department shall develop a state plan and application for funding public
448 preschool programs and shall disburse federal funds as authorized and required by
459 applicable federal law. Beginning in fiscal year 2015-16, the department shall provide any
560 federal funds received to qualified school administrative units as part of the start-up funding
571 provided for the development and operation of public preschool programs under this
582 section. If federal funds are used as part of the start-up funds to operate new or expanded
593 public preschool programs, the students enrolled in these programs must be considered
1504 subsidizable pupils for purposes of state subsidy calculations pursuant to chapter 606‑B,
1515 except that students who have not attained 4 years of age and have not been identified as a
1526 child with a disability pursuant to section 7001, subsection 1-B may not be considered
1537 subsidizable pupils for the purposes of state subsidy calculations made pursuant to chapter
1548 606-B.
15 Sec. 11. 20-A MRSA §4502, sub-§9, as amended by PL 2013, c. 581, §5, is further
16 amended to read:
17 9. Public preschool programs for children 3 or 4 years of age. To the extent the
18 State provides adequate start-up funding for a public preschool program for children 3 or 4
19 years of age, a school administrative unit that does not have a public preschool program for
20 children 3 or 4 years of age may develop a public preschool program implementation plan
21 for children 3 or 4 years of age for submission to and approval by the department.
22 Evaluation and approval of the proposal must include consideration of at least the following
23 factors:
24 A. Demonstrated coordination with other early childhood programs in the community
25 to maximize resources;
26 B. Consideration of the extended child care needs of working parents; and
27 C. Provision of public notice regarding the proposal to the community being served,
28 including the extent to which public notice has been disseminated broadly to other early
29 childhood programs in the community.
30 Beginning with the 2015-2016 school year until the 2026-2027 school year, the
31 commissioner may provide start-up funding as set forth in section 4271 to school
32 administrative units to implement or expand public preschool programs for children 4 years
33 of age as required under this subsection. Beginning with the 2026-2027 school year, the
34 commissioner may provide start-up funding as set forth in section 4271 to school
35 administrative units to implement or expand public preschool programs for children 3 or 4
36 years of age as required under this subsection.
37 Sec. 12. 20-A MRSA §5201, sub-§2, ¶C, as amended by PL 2007, c. 141, §12, is
38 further amended to read:
39 C. A person who will be at least 4 years of age on October 15th of the school year may
40 enroll in a public preschool program prior to kindergarten if it is offered available.
41 Sec. 13. 20-A MRSA §5201, sub-§2, ¶D is enacted to read:
42 D. A person who will be at least 3 years of age on October 15th of the school year and
43 is not identified as a child with a disability pursuant to section 7001, subsection 1-B,
Page 3 - 132LR2618(01)
414 may enroll in a public preschool program if it is available and the program provides
425 instruction to children who are 3 years of age.
3 Sec. 14. 20-A MRSA §5201, sub-§2, ¶E is enacted to read:
4 E. A person who is at least 3 years of age and is identified as a child with a disability
5 pursuant to section 7001, subsection 1-B may enroll in an available public preschool
6 program at any time prior to enrolling in kindergarten.
7 Sec. 15. 20-A MRSA §15688-A, sub-§4, as enacted by PL 2013, c. 581, §10, is
8 amended to read:
9 4. New or expanded public preschool programs for children 4 years of age.
10 Beginning in fiscal year 2015-16 and for each subsequent fiscal year until the 2026-27
11 fiscal year, the commissioner may expend and disburse one-time, start-up funds to provide
12 grants for expanded access to public preschool programs for children 4 years of age
13 pursuant to chapter 203, subchapter 3. Beginning in fiscal year 2026-27 and for each
14 subsequent fiscal year, the commissioner may expend and disburse one-time, start-up funds
15 to provide grants for expanded access to public preschool programs for children 3 or 4
16 years of age pursuant to chapter 203, subchapter 3. The amounts of the grant funding
17 provided to qualified school administrative units pursuant to chapter 203, subchapter 3 are
18 limited to the amounts appropriated, allocated or authorized by the Legislature for the
19 operation of public preschool programs. Any balance of funds appropriated, allocated or
20 authorized by the Legislature remaining at the end of a fiscal year do not lapse and are
21 carried forward to the next fiscal year to carry out the purposes of chapter 203, subchapter
22 3.
23 SUMMARY
2244 This bill expands the definition of "public preschool program" to include a program
2255 that provides instruction to children who are 3 years of age. The bill provides that, in
2266 addition to allowing start-up funds for the operation of public preschools for children who
2277 are 4 years of age, the State, beginning in the 2026-2027 school year, may provide start-up
2288 funding for the allowable costs to operate public preschool programs for children who are
2299 both 3 years of age and identified as a child with a disability. The bill specifies that public
3300 preschool students who are 3 years of age and have not been identified as a child with a
3311 disability are not subsidizable pupils for the purposes of state subsidy calculations under
3322 the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 20-A, chapter 606-B. Under the bill, a child who is 3
3333 years of age on October 15th and does not have an identified disability may still attend a
3344 public preschool program that offers instruction to children who are 3 years of age. Under
3355 the bill, a person who is at least 3 years of age and is identified as a child with a disability
3366 may enroll in an available public preschool program at any time prior to enrolling in
3377 kindergarten.
