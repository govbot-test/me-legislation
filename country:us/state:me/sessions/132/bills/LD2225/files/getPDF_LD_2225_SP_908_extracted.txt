15 Sec. 4. 10 MRSA §9726 is enacted to read:
16 §9726. Code changes and enforcement
17 Notwithstanding any provision of this chapter to the contrary, beginning January 1,
18 2027, the effective date of any changes made by the board to the Maine Uniform Building
19 and Energy Code or to the codes and standards listed in section 9725 between January 1st
20 and June 30th is December 1st of that same calendar year. The effective date of changes
21 made between July 1st and December 31st is June 1st of the next calendar year.
22 Sec. 5. 25 MRSA §2450-A, as amended by PL 2025, c. 388, Pt. D, §31, is further
23 amended to read:
24 §2450-A. Surcharge on plan review fee for Uniform Building Codes and Standards
25 Fund
26 In addition to the fees established in section 2450, a surcharge of 4¢ 6¢ per square foot
27 of occupied space must be levied on the existing fee schedule for new construction,
28 reconstruction, repairs, renovations or new use for the sole purpose of funding the activities
29 of the Technical Building Codes and Standards Board with respect to the Maine Uniform
30 Building and Energy Code, established pursuant to Title 10, chapter 1103, the activities of
31 the Division of Building Codes and Standards under chapter 314 and the activities of the
32 Maine Office of Community Affairs under Title 30‑A, section 4451, subsection 3‑A, except
33 that the fee for review of a plan for the renovation of a public school, including the fee
34 established under section 2450, may not exceed $450. Revenue collected from this
35 surcharge must be deposited into the Uniform Building Codes and Standards Fund
36 established by section 2374.
37 Sec. 6. 30-A MRSA §4451, sub-§3, as amended by PL 2025, c. 388, Pt. D, §38, is
38 further amended to read:
39 3. Training and certification of code enforcement officers. In cooperation with
40 code enforcement officer professional associations, the Maine Community College System,
41 the Department of Environmental Protection and the Department of Health and Human
Page 1 - 132LR3036(01)
412 Services, except as otherwise provided in paragraph H, the Maine Office of Community
423 Affairs shall establish a continuing education program for individuals engaged in code
434 enforcement. This program must provide training in the technical and legal aspects of code
445 enforcement necessary for certification. The training program must include training to
456 provide familiarity with the laws and ordinances related to the structure and practice of the
467 municipal code enforcement office, municipal planning board and appeals board
478 procedures, application review and permitting procedures, inspection procedures and code
489 enforcement techniques, including enforcement of codes regulating industrialized housing.
590 For the purposes of this subsection, "industrialized housing" means a structure designed to
1501 be used for residential housing that consists of or is constructed using one or more modular
1512 buildings, modular components or panelized systems that is wholly or in substantial part
1523 fabricated or assembled in a manufacturing plant for installation, or assembly and
1534 installation, on a separate building site and has been manufactured in such a manner that
1545 all parts or processes cannot be inspected at the installation site without disassembly of,
1556 damage to or destruction of the structure.
16 H. If funding is not available to support the training and certification program
17 authorized under this subsection, the Maine Office of Community Affairs shall
18 discontinue training and certification activities related to laws and ordinances
19 referenced in subsection 2‑A, paragraphs A and B and shall adopt by routine technical
20 rules under Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2‑A a program to register code enforcement
21 officers that meet training and education qualifications. The Maine Office of
22 Community Affairs shall publish the list of persons registered for code enforcement
23 who have submitted evidence of required qualifications. Persons registered under this
24 paragraph must meet the requirements for training and certification under this
25 subchapter. The Maine Office of Community Affairs shall consult with the
26 Department of Health and Human Services for the purposes of carrying out training
27 and certification activities related to laws and ordinances referenced in subsection 2‑A,
28 paragraphs C and D. Within one month of discontinuation of training and certification
29 under this paragraph, the Maine Office of Community Affairs shall report to the joint
30 standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over appropriations and
31 financial affairs and the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction
32 over state and local government matters a recommendation for funding the training and
33 certification program or for further changes in program requirements.
34 Sec. 7. Regionalized code enforcement pilot project. The Maine Office of
35 Community Affairs, through the Housing Opportunity Program established in the Maine
36 Revised Statutes, Title 5, section 3241, shall conduct a 3-year pilot project to promote,
37 incentivize and support the adoption of regionalized approaches to code enforcement by
38 municipalities. The Maine Office of Community Affairs shall administer the pilot project
39 and establish guidelines for the administration of the pilot project as follows.
40 1. The pilot project must start no later than January 1, 2027 and continue for no more
41 than 3 years.
42 2. The Maine Office of Community Affairs shall conduct an ongoing evaluation of the
43 pilot project to determine the efficacy and effect of adopting a regionalized approach to
44 code enforcement for participating municipalities.
Page 2 - 132LR3036(01)
1 3. No later than January 15th of each year for the duration of the pilot project, and no
2 later than 60 days following the end of the pilot project, the Maine Office of Community
3 Affairs shall submit a report on the status of the pilot project to the joint standing committee
4 of the Legislature having jurisdiction over building code matters. The final report must
5 include, but is not limited to, the total number of participating municipalities; the reported
6 effects of adopting a regionalized approach to code enforcement; any barriers to the success
7 of the pilot project; and recommendations, including any suggested legislation, for adopting
8 a regionalized approach to code enforcement.
9 Upon receipt of any report required by this section, the joint standing committee of the
10 Legislature having jurisdiction over building code matters may report out legislation based
11 on the report to the legislative session in which the report is received.
12 Sec. 8. Transfer from Maine Budget Stabilization Fund; Housing
13 Opportunity Program, Other Special Revenue Funds account. Notwithstanding
14 any provision of law to the contrary, on or before June 30, 2027, the State Controller shall
15 transfer $1,000,000 from the Maine Budget Stabilization Fund within the Department of
16 Administrative and Financial Services to the Housing Opportunity Program, Other Special
17 Revenue Funds account for a pilot project to provide regionalized building code
18 enforcement.

23 SUMMARY
3214 This bill is reported out by the Joint Standing Committee on Housing and Economic
3225 Development pursuant to Resolve 2025, chapter 64. The bill implements the
3236 recommendations of the working group to address regulatory barriers to housing
3247 construction by:
35 1. Adding the International Code Council and Modular Building Institute 1200-2021
36 Standard for Off-site Construction: Planning, Design, Fabrication and Assembly to the list
37 of codes that make up the Maine Uniform Building and Energy Code;
38 2. Establishing consistency for when updates to the Maine Uniform Building and
39 Energy Code and other similar codes go into effect;
40 3. Amending the training requirements for code enforcement officers to include
41 training on industrialized housing;
Page 4 - 132LR3036(01)
1 4. Directing the Maine Office of Community Affairs, through the Housing Opportunity
2 Program, to conduct a 3-year pilot project to promote, incentivize and support the adoption
3 of regionalized approaches to code enforcement by municipalities; and
4 5. Increasing from 4¢ to 6¢ per square foot the surcharge on the plan review fee. The
5 surcharge is deposited into the Uniform Building Codes and Standards Fund.
6 The joint standing committee has not taken a position on the substance of this bill. By
7 reporting this bill out, the committee is not suggesting and does not intend to suggest that
8 it agrees or disagrees with any aspect of this bill. The committee is reporting the bill out
9 for the sole purpose of having a bill printed that can be referred to the committee for an
10 appropriate public hearing and subsequent processing in the normal course. The committee
11 is taking this action to ensure clarity and transparency in the legislative review of the
12 proposals contained in the bill.
