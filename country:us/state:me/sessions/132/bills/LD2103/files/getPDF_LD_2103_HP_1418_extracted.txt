1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 22 MRSA §1832, as enacted by PL 2011, c. 254, §1 and affected by §2, is
3 repealed and the following enacted in its place:
4 §1832. Safety and security in hospitals; cybersecurity
5 1. Safety and security plan. A hospital licensed under this chapter shall, on an annual
6 basis, adopt a safety and security plan to protect the patients, visitors and employees of the
7 hospital from aggressive and violent behavior. The safety and security plan must include
8 a process for hospitals to receive and record incidents and threats of violent behavior
9 occurring at or arising out of employment at the hospital. The safety and security plan must
10 prohibit a representative or employee of the hospital from interfering with a person making
11 a report as provided in the plan.
12 2. Cybersecurity plan. A hospital licensed under this chapter shall adopt and submit
13 to the department a cybersecurity plan. The hospital shall update the plan at least once per
14 year and submit the updated plan to the department. The department shall keep the plan
15 on file.
16 A. As used in this subsection, the following terms have the following meanings.
17 (1) "Cybersecurity" means the process of preventing unauthorized modification,
18 misuse or denial of use, or the unauthorized use, of information that is stored,
19 accessed or transferred from an electronic device to an external recipient.
20 (2) "Cybersecurity intrusion" means an unwanted intrusion into a computer
21 system.
22 B. The cybersecurity plan must include, at a minimum:
23 (1) A provision for the timely notification of a cybersecurity intrusion to
24 appropriate parties, including, but not limited to, law enforcement agencies,
25 hospital patients, health care providers, municipalities and state regulators;
26 (2) A backup communication response provision that ensures continuity of care for
27 patients, including providing for the protection of patients' rights;
28 (3) A provision for triage based on system capacities and patient needs to ensure
29 continuity of care;
30 (4) A provision for the diversion of hospital services, including the provision of
31 transportation services, and regional partnerships with other hospitals to ensure
32 continuity of care;
33 (5) A complaint process for patients who, as a result of a cybersecurity intrusion,
34 are experiencing challenges accessing medical care; and
35 (6) A provision for cybersecurity training for hospital employees and board
36 members and organizations affiliated with the hospital.
37 A hospital licensed under this chapter shall annually perform a test run of the cybersecurity
38 plan to assess the implementation of the plan.
39 3. Rules. The department may adopt routine technical rules as defined in Title 5,
40 chapter 375, subchapter 2-A to implement this section.

1 Sec. 2. 22 MRSA §8752, sub-§4-A, ¶C-1, as enacted by PL 2025, c. 305, Pt. B,
2 §4, is amended to read:
3 C-1. The suicide of a patient within 48 hours of receiving health care services in a
4 health care facility; and
5 Sec. 3. 22 MRSA §8752, sub-§4-A, ¶D, as amended by PL 2025, c. 305, Pt. B,
6 §4, is further amended to read:
7 D. Other serious and preventable events as identified by using the most recent version
8 of a nationally recognized quality forum.; and
9 Sec. 4. 22 MRSA §8752, sub-§4-A, ¶E is enacted to read:
10 E. Cybersecurity intrusions, as defined in section 1832, subsection 2, paragraph A,
11 subparagraph (2), impacting patients' access to medical care.
12 SUMMARY
1133 This bill requires hospitals to adopt and submit to the Department of Health and Human
1144 Services a cybersecurity plan that includes provisions related to notifications,
1155 communications, continuity of care for patients and patient complaint processes in the
1166 event of a cybersecurity intrusion. The cybersecurity plan must include a provision for
1177 cybersecurity training for hospital employees and board members and organizations
1188 affiliated with the hospital. The bill also adds cybersecurity intrusions impacting patients'
1199 access to medical care to the definition of "sentinel event" in the laws governing required
2200 reporting of sentinel events.
