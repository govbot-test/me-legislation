7 §14. Learning Results Implementation Advisory Committee
8 1. Learning Results Implementation Advisory Committee. The Learning Results
9 Implementation Advisory Committee, referred to in this section as "the committee," is
10 established in Title 5, section 12004-I, subsection 18-H to make recommendations to the
11 department on the development of curricula, resources and staff training related to the
12 implementation of learning results in the State.
13 2. Membership and staffing. The committee is composed of 10 members appointed
14 by the commissioner. The commissioner shall endeavor to appoint members who represent
15 the racial and ethnic populations in subsection 3, paragraph A. The chair of the committee
16 must be elected from among the committee membership by the members of the committee.
17 The department shall staff at least one position to provide administrative support to the
18 committee.
19 3. Duties of committee. The committee:
20 A. Shall make recommendations to the department regarding the development of
21 curricula, resources and staff training related to the study of the history and culture of
22 racial and ethnic populations in the State. Racial and ethnic populations to be the
23 subject of study under this section include, but are not limited to, Wabanaki, African
24 American, Asian American, Hispanic, Franco-American and Jewish populations;
25 B. Shall make recommendations to the department regarding the development of
26 curricula, resources and staff training related to financial literacy, civics and health
27 care;
28 C. May recommend to the department topics to be measured by learning indicators or
29 adjust learning indicators to reflect best practices. For purposes of this paragraph,
30 "learning indicator" means a process to measure and analyze the effectiveness and
31 ability of a learner to engage and progress within a learning environment;
32 D. Shall include in the recommendations under paragraphs A, B and C the use of the
33 department's online, open-source education platform that provides optional resources
34 at no cost to educators in the State on topic areas and themes identified as areas of need
35 by educators; and
36 E. Shall assist the department in developing program guidelines for the distribution of
37 grants under subsection 4.
38 4. Grants. The department shall establish a program to provide grants to school
39 administrative units and nonprofit organizations to implement programming and learning
40 activities based upon the recommendations of the committee under subsection 3 and other
41 learning results activities determined by the department.
