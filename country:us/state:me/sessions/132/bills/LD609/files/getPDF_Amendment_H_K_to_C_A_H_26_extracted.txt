3 Sec. UU-5. 35-A MRSA §3407, sub-§1, as enacted by PL 2023, c. 481, §5, is
4 amended to read:
5 1. Program established. The Maine Offshore Wind Renewable Energy and
6 Economic Development Program, referred to in this section as "the program," is established
7 to further the development and use of offshore wind power projects in the Gulf of Maine.
8 The office, in collaboration with the commission, shall administer the program in
9 accordance with this section and shall ensure that the program is designed to advance the
10 greenhouse gas emissions reduction obligations and climate policies of this State under
11 Title 38, section 576‑A and Title 38, section 577, the renewable energy goals established
12 in section 3210, subsection 1‑A and all applicable workforce development efforts of this
13 State. Under the program, the office, in collaboration with the commission, shall:
14 A. Encourage the responsible development of offshore wind power projects to stabilize
15 energy supply prices, reduce this State's reliance on imported fossil fuels, realize direct
16 and near-term economic benefits for the people of this State and ensure the
17 achievement of this State's wind energy generation goal established in section 3404,
18 subsection 2;
19 B. Support offshore wind power projects that result in long-term, family-supporting
20 careers, including for disadvantaged populations and communities;
21 C. Support responsibly sited and operated offshore wind power projects that avoid or
22 minimize and compensate for impacts to wildlife, habitat, fisheries and coastal
23 communities in the Gulf of Maine;
24 D. Support efforts to increase understanding of impacts of offshore wind power
25 projects on the marine environment and to advance research to better understand how
26 offshore wind power projects can coexist with minimal impact to existing ocean users,
27 wildlife, fisheries and the marine environment;
28 E. Promote diversity, equity and inclusion in the development of offshore wind power
29 projects with particular consideration given to the energy and economic circumstances
30 and opportunities in coastal and socially vulnerable communities and for the federally
31 recognized Indian tribes in this State. For the purposes of this paragraph, "socially
32 vulnerable communities" means those communities containing populations that are
33 disproportionately burdened by existing social inequities or lack the capacity to
34 withstand new or worsening burdens;
35 F. Support the advancement of port infrastructure, local offshore wind power-related
36 economic development and the development of a supply chain to support the
37 development of offshore wind power projects;
38 G. Support the development and deployment of innovative technologies to achieve the
39 energy policy and economic development objectives of this State, including energy
40 storage;
41 H. Support the use of Maine-based technical and engineering expertise and build on
42 this State's leadership in offshore wind power, maritime industries and research and
43 development;
