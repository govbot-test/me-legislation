4 STATE OF MAINE
5 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
6 132ND LEGISLATURE
7 FIRST REGULAR SESSION
8 HOUSE AMENDMENT “ ” to COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “A” to H.P. 131,
9 L.D. 209, “An Act to Make Supplemental Appropriations and Allocations from the General
10 Fund and Other Funds for the Expenditures of State Government and to Change Certain
11 Provisions of the Law Necessary to the Proper Operations of State Government for the
12 Fiscal Year Ending June 30, 2025”
13 Amend the amendment by inserting after Part X the following:
14 'PART Y
15 Sec. Y-1. 26 MRSA §850-A, sub-§13, as enacted by PL 2023, c. 412, Pt. AAA,
16 §7, is amended to read:
17 13. Employee. "Employee" means a person who may be permitted, required or
18 directed by an employer participating in the program in consideration of direct or indirect
19 gain or profit to engage in any employment in the State but does not include an independent
20 contractor.
21 Sec. Y-2. 26 MRSA §850-A, sub-§14, as enacted by PL 2023, c. 412, Pt. AAA,
22 §7, is amended to read:
23 14. Employer. "Employer" means an employer that chooses to participate in the
24 program and that is:
25 A. Any person, sole proprietorship, partnership, corporation, association or other
26 business entity that employs employees at a location in this State;
27 B. The State, including the executive, legislative and judicial branches, and a state
28 department or agency;
29 C. A county, city, town or municipal agency;
30 D. An agent of an employer, the State or a political subdivision of the State;
31 E. A public employer, as defined in section 962, subsection 7; and
32 F. A tribal government that has elected coverage pursuant to section 850‑G.
33 "Employer" does not include the Federal Government.'
34 Amend the amendment by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or
35 section number to read consecutively.

HOUSE AMENDMENT “ ” to COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “A” to H.P. 131, L.D. 209
1 SUMMARY
22 This amendment amends the paid family and medical leave law to allow an employer
33 to choose to participate in the paid family and medical leave benefits program and specifies
44 that only an employee of a participating employer is eligible for benefits under the paid
55 family and medical leave law.
