Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
SECOND REGULAR SESSION-2026
Legislative Document No. 2019
An Act to Amend the Laws Governing Licensure of Wholesalers and
Manufacturers Under the Maine Pharmacy Act
(EMERGENCY)
Submitted by the Department of Professional and Financial Regulation pursuant to Joint
Rule 203.
Received by the Clerk of the House on December 3, 2025. Referred to the Committee on
Health Coverage, Insurance and Financial Services pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered
printed pursuant to Joint Rule 401.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative MATHIESON of Kittery.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3 Whereas, wholesalers and manufacturers doing business in this State are currently
4 required to submit registration information from the United States Department of Justice,
5 Drug Enforcement Administration and registration information from the United States
6 Food and Drug Administration to the Maine Board of Pharmacy as a qualification for initial
7 licensure; and
8 Whereas, this legislation is necessary to make statutory corrections authorizing the
9 Maine Board of Pharmacy to grant licenses to wholesalers and manufacturers who have
10 not yet obtained the appropriate federal registration number at the time of application for
11 initial licensure; and
12 Whereas, this legislation must take effect before the end of the 90-day period to
13 address, as soon as possible, the health and welfare of the citizens of this State; and
14 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
15 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
16 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
17 therefore,
18 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
19 Sec. 1. 32 MRSA §13742-A, sub-§1, ¶F, as amended by PL 2021, c. 303, §3, is
20 further amended to read:
21 F. A violation of section 13800‑B; or
22 Sec. 2. 32 MRSA §13742-A, sub-§1, ¶G, as enacted by PL 2021, c. 303, §4, is
23 amended to read:
24 G. A violation of section 13725.; or
25 Sec. 3. 32 MRSA §13742-A, sub-§1, ¶H is enacted to read:
26 H. Failing to file with the board an applicable registration number described in section
27 13758, subsection 4, paragraph B once that applicable registration number is obtained.
28 Sec. 4. 32 MRSA §13758, sub-§4, as amended by PL 2007, c. 402, Pt. DD, §28,
29 is repealed and the following enacted in its place:
30 4. Form. License forms must state:
31 A. The applicant's name, address, day phone, 24-hour phone, ownership status and
32 manufacturer or wholesaler designation;
33 B. The applicant's United States Department of Justice, Drug Enforcement
34 Administration registration number and the applicant's United States Food and Drug
35 Administration registration number, as applicable, if the applicant has obtained the
36 registration number at the time the form is executed; and
37 C. The date the form is executed.
38 License forms must be executed by an owner or officer of the manufacturer or wholesaler,
39 providing printed name and title.
Page 1 - 132LR2818(01)
1 If, at the time the form is executed, the applicant has not obtained a registration number
2 described in paragraph B that is applicable to the applicant, the applicant shall file that
3 registration number with the board once the registration number is obtained.
4 Emergency clause. In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation
5 takes effect when approved.
6 SUMMARY
77 This bill modifies the initial licensure qualifications for prescription drug
88 manufacturers and wholesalers by allowing an applicant to apply for licensure before the
99 applicant has obtained a registration number from the appropriate federal agencies but
1100 requires that a manufacturer or wholesaler licensed in this State file the applicable
1111 registration numbers with the Maine Board of Pharmacy once the registration numbers are
1122 obtained by the manufacturer or wholesaler. The bill also provides that the board may deny
1133 a license, refuse to renew a license or impose disciplinary sanctions if the manufacturer or
1144 wholesaler fails to file the applicable registration numbers with the board once the
1155 registration numbers are obtained.
