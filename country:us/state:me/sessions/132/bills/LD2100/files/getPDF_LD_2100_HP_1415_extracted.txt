1 SUMMARY
22 Current law requires that every room containing an appliance that combusts fuel gas in
33 each unit in any building of multifamily occupancy have a fuel gas detector. This bill
44 requires that every room containing an appliance that combusts fuel gas in each unit in any
55 building of multifamily occupancy with 3 or more dwelling units and each apartment in an
66 apartment building have a fuel gas detector. Current law requires that every room
77 containing an appliance that combusts fuel gas in a hotel, motel or inn have a fuel gas
88 detector. The bill requires that every room containing an appliance that combusts fuel gas
99 in a lodging or rooming house, hotel or dormitory as defined in the National Fire Protection
1100 Association Life Safety Code or every room containing an appliance that combusts fuel
1111 gas in a motel or inn have a fuel gas detector. Current law requires that beginning January
1122 1, 2026, every room containing an appliance that combusts fuel gas in a business
1133 occupancy, a mercantile occupancy and an assembly occupancy have a fuel gas detector.
1144 The bill requires that beginning January 1, 2027, every room containing an appliance that
1155 combusts fuel gas in a business occupancy, a mercantile occupancy and an assembly
1166 occupancy as defined in the National Fire Protection Association Life Safety Code have a
1177 fuel gas detector. The bill also requires that beginning January 1, 2028, every room
1188 containing an appliance that combusts fuel gas in a day care occupancy, an educational
1199 occupancy, a health care occupancy, an ambulatory health care occupancy, a residential
2200 board and care occupancy and a detention and correctional occupancy as defined in the
2211 National Fire Protection Association Life Safety Code have a fuel gas detector. Finally,
2222 the bill provides that the laws governing fuel gas detectors may be enforced by the Office
2233 of the State Fire Marshal; a state, county or municipal law enforcement officer; a municipal
2244 code enforcement officer; or a state, county or municipal fire inspector.
