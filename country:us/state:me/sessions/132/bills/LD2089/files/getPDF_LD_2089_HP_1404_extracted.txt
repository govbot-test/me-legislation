Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
SECOND REGULAR SESSION-2026
Legislative Document No. 2089
An Act to Amend the Law Governing Agricultural Labor Housing
Standards
Submitted by the Department of Labor pursuant to Joint Rule 203.
Reference to the Committee on Labor suggested and ordered printed.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative PLUECKER of Warren.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 26 MRSA §586, as enacted by PL 1997, c. 387, §2, is amended to read:
3 §586. Agricultural labor housing standards
4 The bureau shall adopt rules for the protection of the health, safety and welfare of the
5 agricultural laborers and their families who occupy housing provided, owned or controlled
6 by their employers. These rules apply only to housing facilities of employers of agricultural
7 labor who provide housing to more than 5 employees and whose minimum housing
8 habitability standards are not already established under the regulations on housing
9 promulgated by the United States Department of Labor, Occupational Safety and Health
10 Administration under the federal Migrant and Seasonal Agricultural Worker Protection
11 Act, 29 United States Code, Sections 1801 et seq. The rules adopted under this subchapter
12 must be identical to the federal housing habitability regulations promulgated to protect
13 seasonal and migrant workers under the authority of the federal Migrant and Seasonal
14 Agricultural Worker Protection Act. Rules adopted pursuant to this subchapter section are
15 routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter II‑A 2-A.
16 SUMMARY
1177 This bill removes the requirement that rules adopted by the Department of Labor,
1188 Bureau of Labor Standards governing agricultural labor housing standards must be
1199 identical to the federal housing habitability regulations promulgated to protect seasonal and
2200 migrant workers under the authority of the federal Migrant and Seasonal Agricultural
2211 Worker Protection Act.
