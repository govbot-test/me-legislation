10 CHAPTER 39
11 ONLINE SWEEPSTAKES GAMES PROHIBITED
12 §1401. Definitions
13 As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms
14 have the following meanings.
15 1. Board. "Board" means the Gambling Control Board established under section 1002.
16 2. Direct consideration. "Direct consideration" means a coin, token or other
17 representation of value that may be purchased by a person or received through a bonus or
18 promotion and that is used for playing or participating in an online sweepstakes game.
19 3. Dual-currency system of payment. "Dual-currency system of payment" means a
20 system of payment that allows a person to play or participate with direct consideration or
21 indirect consideration or that is otherwise designed in a manner that:
22 A. Prevents a person from directly purchasing coins, tokens or other representations of
23 value that are redeemable for any cash prize, cash award or cash equivalents or the
24 chance to win any cash prize, cash award or cash equivalents; and
25 B. Encourages a person to purchase services, products, coins, tokens or other
26 representations of value that are not exchangeable for a prize, award, cash or cash
27 equivalents or a chance to win a prize, award, cash or cash equivalents in order for that
28 person to obtain the coins, tokens or other representations of value that are
29 exchangeable for prizes, awards, cash or cash equivalents or a chance to win a prize,
30 award, cash or cash equivalents.
31 The unit may adopt rules to further define what constitutes a dual-currency system of
32 payment. Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are major substantive rules as defined
33 in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2‑A.
34 4. Indirect consideration. “Indirect consideration” means a coin, token or other
35 representation of value that:
36 A. Is provided for free through a promotion or bonus or with the purchase of a related
37 product, service or activity; and

12 A. Is available on the Internet and accessible on a mobile telephone, computer terminal

14 B. Uses a dual-currency system of payment; and
15 C. Simulates casino-style gaming, including, but not limited to, slot machines, poker
16 and other table games, lottery games, bingo or sports wagering.
17 7. Unit. "Unit" means the Gambling Control Unit established as a bureau within the
18 Department of Public Safety under Title 25, section 2902, subsection 12.
19 §1402. Online sweepstakes games prohibited; fines; other penalties
20 A person that operates or promotes an online sweepstakes game or supports the
21 operation or promotion of an online sweepstakes game commits a civil violation.
22 1. Fine. A person that violates this section is subject to a fine of not less than $10,000
23 and not more than $100,000. All fines adjudged pursuant to this section must be collected
24 by the unit and distributed to the Gambling Addiction Prevention and Treatment Fund
25 established in Title 5, section 20006-B.
26 2. Revocation of license. If a licensee is convicted of unlawful gambling under Title
27 17-A, section 954, subsection 1-B or is otherwise found to be in violation of this section
28 through the administrative process applicable to the license held by the licensee, the unit
29 or board, whichever is responsible for administering the license, shall revoke the licensee's
30 license.
31 3. Ineligible for license. A person that is found to be in violation of this section is
32 ineligible to receive a license described in section 1401, subsection 5.

1 SUMMARY
22 This bill clarifies that operating or promoting an online sweepstakes game constitutes
33 "unlawful gambling" within the meaning of the Maine Criminal Code and establishes
44 additional civil penalties related to that unlawful conduct.
5 The bill impacts online games or contests that use a dual-currency system of payment
6 and that simulate casino-style gaming, including, but not limited to, slot machines, poker
7 and other table games, lottery games, bingo or sports wagering. "Dual-currency system of
8 payment" refers to the practice of using more than one system of coins or tokens to facilitate
9 game play and is further defined in the bill.
10 The bill provides that a person that operates or promotes an online sweepstakes game
11 is subject to a fine of not less than $10,000 and not more than $100,000. The bill requires
12 that all fines collected be distributed to the Gambling Addiction Prevention and Treatment
13 Fund.
14 Under the bill, if a person is found to operate or promote an online sweepstakes game
15 and that person holds a license with the Gambling Control Unit or Gambling Control Board
16 within the Department of Public Safety to operate certain gambling activities, the licensing
17 entity must revoke that license. A person that violates the online sweepstakes games
18 prohibition is also ineligible to receive one of the licenses specified by the bill.
