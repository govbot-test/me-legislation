Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
SECOND REGULAR SESSION-2026
Legislative Document No. 2076
An Act to Amend the Laws Governing the Reporting of Incidents by
Municipal Fire Chiefs
Submitted by the Department of Public Safety pursuant to Joint Rule 203.
Received by the Clerk of the House on December 15, 2025. Referred to the Committee on
Criminal Justice and Public Safety pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed pursuant to
Joint Rule 401.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative BUNKER of Farmington.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 25 MRSA §2395, as amended by PL 2007, c. 82, §2, is further amended to
3 read:
4 §2395. Filing statement Incident report of fire or other occurrence
5 The municipal fire chief or a designee shall submit to the State Fire Marshal an incident
6 report for each response made, regardless of whether an actual fire occurred. The Until
7 January 1, 2026, the report must be submitted in a manner consistent with a national fire
8 incident reporting system or, as available, a national emergency response information
9 system. On or after January 1, 2026, the report must be submitted in a manner consistent
10 with a national emergency response information system or a successor system as
11 determined by the United States Department of Homeland Security, Federal Emergency
12 Management Agency, United States Fire Administration.
13 Sec. 2. Rules. The State Fire Marshal within the Department of Public Safety may
14 adopt rules to collect statewide incident response data and facilitate the transition from a
15 national fire incident reporting system to a national emergency response information
16 system, including provisions for electronic submission, technical assistance and system
17 integration with municipal reporting systems. Rules adopted pursuant to this section are
18 routine technical rules as described in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 375,
19 subchapter 2-A.
20 Sec. 3. Retroactivity. That section of this Act that amends the Maine Revised
21 Statutes, Title 25, section 2395 applies retroactively to January 1, 2026.
22 SUMMARY
2233 This bill requires that, until January 1, 2026, incident reports submitted by municipal
2244 fire chiefs to the State Fire Marshal within the Department of Public Safety be submitted
2255 in a manner consistent with a national fire incident reporting system or, as available, a
2266 national emergency response information system. On or after January 1, 2026, those
2277 reports must be submitted in a manner consistent with a national emergency response
2288 information system or a successor system as determined by the United States Department
2299 of Homeland Security, Federal Emergency Management Agency, United States Fire
3300 Administration. These provisions apply retroactively to January 1, 2026. The bill also
3311 authorizes the State Fire Marshal to adopt rules to collect statewide incident response data
3322 and facilitate the transition from a national fire incident reporting system to a national
3333 emergency response information system.
