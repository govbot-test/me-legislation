An Act to Establish a Monetary Penalty for Employers Whose
Unemployment Payment Is Returned Unpaid
Submitted by the Department of Labor pursuant to Joint Rule 203.
Reference to the Committee on Labor suggested and ordered printed.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative ROEDER of Bangor.
Printed on recycled paper

1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 26 MRSA §1225, sub-§9 is enacted to read:
3 9. Returned payments. If any payment made under this chapter is returned unpaid
4 for any reason, including but not limited to insufficient funds, account closure,
5 nonexistence of the account, stop-payment order or any other cause, the commissioner shall
6 assess a penalty equal to $25 or 1% of the payment amount, whichever is greater.
7 SUMMARY
88 This bill provides that if a payment made under the Employment Security Law is
99 returned for any reason, including but not limited to insufficient funds, account closure,
1100 nonexistence of the account or stop-payment order, the Commissioner of Labor must assess
1111 a penalty equal to $25 or 1% of the payment amount, whichever is greater.
