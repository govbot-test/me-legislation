16 §12823. Endangered plant species and threatened plant species
17 The commissioner has the following responsibilities related to endangered plant
18 species and threatened plant species.
19 1. Official list of endangered plant species and threatened plant species. The
20 commissioner shall establish and maintain an official list of endangered plant species and
21 threatened plant species of the State. The purpose of the list is informational and may be
22 provided on an informational basis to public agencies, private institutions or individuals
23 for environmental assessment, land management or educational purposes.
24 2. Identification procedures. The commissioner may establish procedures to
25 substantiate the identification of endangered plant species and threatened plant species. In
26 determining and revising the official list under subsection 1, the commissioner shall use
27 the department's rare plant database and the knowledge of botanists in the State. In addition,
28 the commissioner shall consult with federal agencies, interested state agencies, other states
29 or provinces having a common interest and other interested persons and organizations. The
30 commissioner shall determine criteria for each category. When establishing the list, the
31 commissioner shall consider aspects of plant biology that contribute to a species' rarity,
32 such as:
33 A. Endemism. A plant species or subspecies may be geographically restricted to the
34 State or areas immediately adjacent to the State;
35 B. Scarcity. A plant species or subspecies may be scarce throughout its distribution in
36 North America and occur in only a few locations in the State;
37 C. Special habitat. A plant species or subspecies may require habitat that is scarce in
38 the State;
39 D. Limit of range. A plant species or subspecies in the State may be at the edge of its
40 distribution or disjunct from its main distribution; and
Page 14 - 132LR2633(01)

1 E. Population decline or vulnerability. A plant species or subspecies may be threatened
2 or seriously declining due to habitat modification or destruction or from overcollection
3 for commercial, recreational or educational purposes.
4 3. Public hearing. The commissioner shall conduct at least one public hearing to
5 allow for public comment before establishing or revising the official list under subsection
6 1.
7 4. Review of inventory. The commissioner shall review the official list under
8 subsection 1 biennially and add or delete a plant species or subspecies based on new
9 botanical inventory data, taxonomic or other scientific studies or other documentation.
10 §12824. Sensitive information
11 The commissioner may withhold specific information on the location of a plant species,
12 subspecies or natural area and its component features if, in the judgment of the
13 commissioner, disclosure of this information would threaten the existence of that species
14 or subspecies or natural area. The commissioner may not deny a landowner or landowner's
15 designee information about species, subspecies or natural areas occurring on the
16 landowner's property or withhold this information from usual environmental review
17 procedures of local, state or federal regulatory agencies.
18 §12825. Rulemaking
19 The commissioner may not adopt rules to protect plants and plant communities under
20 this subchapter except when required for the conservation of rare fish or wildlife species,
21 threatened fish or wildlife species or endangered fish or wildlife species.
