1 Sec. 1. Meat and poultry inspection study. Resolved: That the Department of
2 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry shall conduct a review of programs and processes
3 related to meat and poultry inspections, including processes related to exemptions from
4 inspections, to determine areas for possible improvements, efficiencies and enhanced
5 flexibility to benefit agricultural producers, processors and retail and public customers. In
6 conducting the review under this section, the department shall consult with livestock and
7 poultry producers and processors, the United States Department of Agriculture, the
8 University of Maine Cooperative Extension and other stakeholders that the department
9 determines necessary to assess existing programs' statutory authorities, rules, regulations,
10 policies and practices, including those involving investigations, enforcement and staff
11 training. The department shall also review the existing exemptions that relate to poultry
12 processors that process certain numbers of poultry per year and the department's existing
13 cooperative agreement with the United States Department of Agriculture, Food Safety and
14 Inspection Service and that agreement's related requirements.
15 Sec. 2. Report. Resolved: That, by January 15, 2026, the Department of
16 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry shall submit a report based on its review in section
17 1, including recommended state or federal legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on
18 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry. The Joint Standing Committee on Agriculture,
19 Conservation and Forestry may submit legislation related to the report to the Second
20 Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.
