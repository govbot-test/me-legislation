1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 12 MRSA §6089 is enacted to read:
3 §6089. Scallop biotoxin testing
4 1. Scallop biotoxin testing. As part of the Marine Shellfish Toxins Monitoring
5 Program under section 6076, the department shall conduct testing of cultured scallops for
6 biotoxins in the same manner as the testing for other cultured mollusks under the program,
7 including using high-performance liquid chromatography testing. The department shall
8 provide logistical support to a commercial fisheries business engaged in scallop
9 aquaculture in conducting the testing under this section, including providing transportation
10 of samples to be tested. For purposes of this section, "high-performance liquid
11 chromatography testing" means a testing technique in analytical chemistry that uses high-
12 pressure pumps to separate, identify and quantify specific components in mixtures.
13 2. Funding. The department shall carry out the purposes of this section through
14 appropriations, allocations, federal grants, donations from public or private sources or a
15 partnership with an organization having the purpose of supporting marine aquaculture.
16 Funds received under this subsection must be used for biotoxin testing and logistical
17 support for commercial fisheries businesses under subsection 1.
18 3. Report. By January 1st of each year, the department shall submit an annual report
19 detailing the number of commercial fisheries businesses using the testing under this section,
20 the overall impact on the scallop aquaculture industry, including economic growth and
21 cultured scallops landings, and recommendations for further improvements or expansions
22 of the testing under this section to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having
23 jurisdiction over marine resources matters.
24 SUMMARY
2255 This bill requires the Department of Marine Resources to conduct biotoxin testing of
2266 cultured scallops under the Marine Shellfish Toxins Monitoring Program. Testing
2277 requirements include using high-performance liquid chromatography testing and providing
2288 logistical support to commercial fisheries businesses engaged in scallop aquaculture,
2299 including providing transportation of samples for testing. Funds received by the department
3300 for the testing may be used only for biotoxin testing of cultured scallops, and the department
3311 is required to submit an annual report on the testing to the Legislature by January 1st of
3322 each year.
