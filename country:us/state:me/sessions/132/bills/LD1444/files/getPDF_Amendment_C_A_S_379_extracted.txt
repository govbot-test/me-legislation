14 Amend the bill by striking out all of section 2 and inserting the following:
15 'Sec. 2. 14 MRSA §6111, sub-§1-B is enacted to read:
16 1-B. Subsequent foreclosure. A subsequent foreclosure action may be based only
17 upon a default of the mortgage that occurred after the entry of foreclosure judgment in
18 favor of the mortgagor and may not seek recovery of any sums due prior to the entry of the
19 foreclosure judgment, including, but not limited to, payments due on principal, interest,
20 property taxes and insurance required by the mortgage. In addition, in a subsequent
21 foreclosure action, the foreclosing party is not entitled to seek recovery of fees, including
22 attorney's fees, or any other costs associated with the prosecution of the foreclosure action
23 that resulted in a dismissal or judgment in favor of the defendant. A violation of this
24 subsection prevents the foreclosing party, or any assignee of the foreclosing party, from
25 instituting a subsequent foreclosure action on the mortgage that is the subject of the
26 foreclosure action.
27 Sec. 3. Retroactive application. The holding in the case of Finch v. U.S. Bank,
28 N.A., 2024 Me. 2 may not be applied retroactively, except in cases in which a final
29 judgment as to the issue of its retroactive application has been entered prior to the effective
30 date of this Act.
31 Sec. 4. Rulemaking. The judicial branch shall adopt rules to implement this Act.'
32 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
33 number to read consecutively.
