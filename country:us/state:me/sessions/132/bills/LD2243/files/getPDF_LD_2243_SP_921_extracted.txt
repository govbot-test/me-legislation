Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
SECOND REGULAR SESSION-2026
Legislative Document No. 2243
S.P. 921 In Senate, April 6, 2026
(EMERGENCY)
Reference to the Committee on Energy, Utilities and Technology suggested and ordered
printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator MARTIN of Oxford. (GOVERNOR'S BILL)
Printed on recycled paper
1 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3 Whereas, the West Paris Water District is under an administrative order from the
4 Maine drinking water program to replace a water storage tank by December 31, 2027; and
5 Whereas, the needed funds exceed the current borrowing capacity of the district; and
6 Whereas, the district needs an immediate increase in its borrowing authority in order
7 to timely schedule the needed improvements; and
8 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
9 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
10 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
11 therefore,
12 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
13 Sec. 1. P&SL 1963, c. 83, §10, first ¶, as amended by P&SL 1995, c. 8, §1, is
14 further amended to read:
15 Sec. 10. Authorized to borrow money, to issue bonds and notes. For
16 accomplishing the purposes of this act, and for such other expenses as may be necessary
17 for the carrying out of these purposes, the district, through its trustees, without vote of the
18 inhabitants, is authorized to borrow money temporarily and to issue therefor its negotiable
19 notes; and for the purpose of renewing and refunding the indebtedness so created, of paying
20 any necessary expenses and liabilities incurred under the provisions of this act, including
21 organizational and other necessary expenses and liabilities whether incurred by the district
22 or the Town of West Paris, the district being authorized to reimburse the Town of West
23 Paris for any such expense incurred by it and in acquiring properties, paying damages,
24 laying pipes, mains, aqueducts and conduits, constructing, maintaining and operating a
25 water plant or system and making renewals, additions, extensions and improvements to the
26 same and to cover interest payments during the period of construction, the district, through
27 its trustees, without the vote of its inhabitants, is also authorized to issue, from time to time,
28 bonds, notes or other evidences of indebtedness of the district in one series, or in separate
29 series, in such amount or amounts, bearing interest at such rate or rates, and having such
30 terms and provisions as the trustees determine; provided except that the total outstanding
31 indebtedness of the district may not exceed $1,000,000 $8,000,000 at any one time.
32 Emergency clause. In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation
33 takes effect when approved.
34 SUMMARY
3355 This bill increases the borrowing limit of the West Paris Water District from
3366 $1,000,000 to $8,000,000.
