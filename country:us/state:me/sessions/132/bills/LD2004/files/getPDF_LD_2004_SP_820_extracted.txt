1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 7 MRSA §219-B, as corrected by RR 2021, c. 1, Pt. A, §7, is amended to
3 read:
4 §219-B. Fund To Address Food Insecurity and Provide Nutrition Incentives
5 1. Fund established. The Fund To Address Food Insecurity and Provide Nutrition
6 Incentives, referred to in this section as "the fund," is established in the department to
7 provide incentives to federal food and nutrition assistance program participants for the
8 purchase of locally grown fruits and vegetables and to support outreach for and
9 administration of programs that offer nutrition incentives to participants of federal food and
10 nutrition assistance programs. The fund is established to match contributions from private
11 and public sources of up to $50,000 annually to further the purposes of this section. The
12 fund, to be accounted within the department, must be held separate and apart from all other
13 money, funds and accounts. Eligible investment earnings credited to the assets of the fund
14 become part of the assets of the fund. Any unexpended balances remaining in the fund at
15 the end of any fiscal year do not lapse and must be carried forward to the next fiscal year.
16 For purposes of this section, "local" means within the State.
17 2. Fund recipients. An organization based in the State that supports local food
18 producers, local food production or low-income individuals in receiving food and nutrition
19 assistance may receive proceeds from the fund upon application with the department. The
20 department shall prioritize an applicant that has a demonstrated history of incentivizing the
21 use of federal food and nutrition assistance programs to purchase locally grown fruits and
22 vegetables or that demonstrates the ability to leverage the proceeds to match or receive
23 additional funds from local, state, federal or private sources. The department shall
24 periodically post a request for applications for eligible organizations to apply for fund
25 proceeds.
26 3. Report; audit. The department shall require a periodic report from a recipient
27 under subsection 2 detailing the use of fund proceeds and the federal food and nutrition
28 assistance programs involved and to ensure that the funds are expended appropriately
29 pursuant to this section. The department may audit a recipient to carry out the purposes of
30 this subsection.
31 4. Rules. The department may adopt routine technical rules as defined in Title 5,
32 chapter 375, subchapter 2‑A to carry out the purposes of the fund, including application
33 criteria and procedures for recipients, disbursement of funds to recipients and for outreach
34 and administration purposes and reporting and audit procedures for recipients.
35 SUMMARY
3366 This bill makes funds from the Fund To Address Food Insecurity and Provide Nutrition
3377 Incentives more readily available to local nutrition incentive programs by removing
3388 language that specifies federal food and nutrition assistance programs.
