132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
SECOND REGULAR SESSION-2026
Legislative Document No. 2006
S.P. 822 In Senate, December 5, 2025
to Make Certain Land Transactions in Aroostook and Somerset
Counties
Submitted by the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry pursuant to Joint
Rule 203.
Received by the Secretary of the Senate on December 3, 2025. Referred to the Committee
on Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator BERNARD of Aroostook.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Preamble. The Constitution of Maine, Article IX, Section 23 requires that real estate
2 held by the State for conservation or recreation purposes may not be reduced or its uses
3 substantially altered except on the vote of 2/3 of all members elected to each House.
4 Whereas, certain real estate authorized for conveyance by this resolve is under the
5 designations described in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 12, section 598-A; and
6 Whereas, the Director of the Bureau of Parks and Lands within the Department of
7 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry may sell or exchange lands with the approval of
8 the Legislature in accordance with the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 12, sections 1814 and
9 1851; now, therefore, be it
10 Sec. 1. Director of Bureau of Parks and Lands authorized to convey certain
11 land in Westmanland, Aroostook County. Resolved: That the Director of the
12 Bureau of Parks and Lands within the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and
13 Forestry may convey, by quitclaim deed with a reverter clause, and at no cost, on other
14 terms and conditions as the director may direct, the State's interest in approximately 7 acres
15 of public reserved land to the Town of Westmanland. The 7 acres represent the section of
16 the Little Madawaska Lake Road that is currently designated as public reserved lands under
17 the bureau. The purpose of the conveyance is to enable the town to improve and maintain
18 this section of road by which several town property taxpayers access their property. The
19 cost of the bureau's attorney's fees, as well as the cost of a survey of the property
20 boundaries, must be paid by the town, and the recording costs may not be paid by the
21 bureau.
22 Sec. 2. Director of Bureau of Parks and Lands authorized to sell certain
23 land in Seboomook Township, Somerset County. Resolved: That the Director of
24 the Bureau of Parks and Lands within the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and
25 Forestry may sell to the owners of 51 Sebey Lane in Big W Township, and no other party,
26 approximately 0.25 acres of public reserved land. The 0.25 acres represent the land under
27 an existing septic system that serves 51 Sebey Lane and was installed prior to state
28 ownership of the land. The buyer must pay all transaction costs, including survey, legal
29 and recording costs for the transaction. The director may negotiate a price for the land or,
30 if needed, obtain an appraisal that must be paid by the buyer.
31 Sec. 3. Director of Bureau of Parks and Lands authorized to exchange
32 certain land in Presque Isle, Aroostook County. Resolved: That the Director of
33 the Bureau of Parks and Lands within the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and
34 Forestry may engage in a minor land exchange of less than 0.25 acres with Towers North,
35 Inc. in the City of Presque Isle. The land swap will rectify a recently identified boundary
36 issue in which a communication tower occupies a very small portion of state land. Towers
37 North, Inc. must pay all transaction costs, including legal and recording costs for the
38 transaction.
39 SUMMARY

4433 of Westmanland to the town. The bureau is also authorized to sell approximately 0.25 acres
