1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 29-A MRSA §1952, sub-§1, as amended by PL 2003, c. 452, Pt. Q, §25
3 and affected by Pt. X, §2, is further amended to read:
4 1. Carry flares Flares and reflective triangles. Except as provided in subsection
5 1‑A, a truck or truck tractor with a registration for operation with gross vehicle weight in
6 excess of 10,000 pounds must be equipped with 2 red flags, 3 flares and 3 red lanterns or
7 red emergency reflectors reflective triangles.
8 Sec. 2. 29-A MRSA §1952, sub-§2, as enacted by PL 1993, c. 683, Pt. A, §2 and
9 affected by Pt. B, §5, is amended to read:
10 2. Disabled vehicle. When a truck or truck tractor with a registration for operation
11 with gross vehicle weight in excess of 10,000 pounds is disabled on a public way, the
12 operator shall, during the time that lights are required to be illuminated, place emergency
13 signals as follows:
14 A. One flare or lantern or red emergency reflector reflective triangle in the center of
15 the lane of traffic occupied by the disabled motor vehicle not less than 100 feet from
16 the vehicle in the direction of traffic approaching in that lane;
17 B. One flare or lantern or red emergency reflector reflective triangle not less than 100
18 feet from the vehicle in the center of the same lane in the opposite direction; and
19 C. One flare or lantern or red emergency reflector reflective triangle at the traffic side
20 of the vehicle not closer than 10 feet from the front or rear.
21 When lights are not required to be illuminated, red flags must be used, except that no flag
22 is required to be placed at the side of the vehicle.
23 SUMMARY
2244 This bill amends the law governing emergency equipment for trucks and truck tractors
2255 by removing references to flags, lanterns and emergency reflectors and replacing them with
2266 references to reflective triangles.
