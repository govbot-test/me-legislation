132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
SECOND REGULAR SESSION-2026
Legislative Document No. 2001
S.P. 815 In Senate, December 5, 2025
Adults Under the Health and Human Services Laws
Submitted by the Department of Health and Human Services pursuant to Joint Rule 203.
Received by the Secretary of the Senate on December 3, 2025. Referred to the Committee
on Health and Human Services pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator INGWERSEN of York.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 22 MRSA §7703, sub-§2, ¶B, as enacted by PL 1983, c. 691, §2, is
3 amended to read:
4 B. Notwithstanding sections 3474 and 4008, any information gathered and all records
5 created or obtained in the course of an investigation of neglect or abuse, including all
6 post-investigation activities and proceedings, except a statement indicating whether or
7 not a report of abuse or neglect has been received, the nature of the alleged abuse or
8 neglect and the conclusion reached by the department, if any;
9 Sec. 2. 22 MRSA §7802, sub-§1-A, as amended by PL 2023, c. 405, Pt. A, §78, is
10 further amended to read:
11 1-A. Consolidation of functions. All staff performing general licensing functions
12 within the Office of Child and Family Services, including the out-of-home abuse and
13 neglect investigating team when investigating pursuant to section 8354, subsection 2,
14 paragraph C 4099-L, are consolidated as a single organizational unit.
15 Sec. 3. 22 MRSA §8103, sub-§2-A, as enacted by PL 2019, c. 444, §3, is amended
16 to read:
17 2-A. Family foster homes and specialized children's homes. Family foster homes
18 and specialized children's homes are exempt from the inspection requirement in subsection
19 1‑A. The department shall inspect a family foster home or specialized children's home
20 prior to placing a foster child in the home. The department shall adopt routine technical
21 rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2‑A governing the fire and safety
22 inspection of family foster homes and specialized children's homes.
23 Sec. 4. 22 MRSA §8104, as amended by PL 2005, c. 12, Pt. RR, §1 and c. 397, Pt.
24 A, §28, is further amended by amending the section headnote to read:
25 §8104. Interagency licensing Licensing authority and rules
26 Sec. 5. 22 MRSA §8104, sub-§1, as amended by PL 2005, c. 397, Pt. A, §28, is
27 repealed.
28 Sec. 6. 22 MRSA §8402, sub-§3, ¶A, as amended by PL 2019, c. 154, §10 and
29 affected by §12 and amended by PL 2025, c. 316, §3, is further amended to read:
30 A. The department shall adopt rules regarding the health of staff as required to protect
31 the health and safety of the children. The rules must include a requirement that every
32 2 years each licensee, administrator or other staff member of the nursery school who
33 provides care for children be declared free from communicable disease by a licensed
34 physician, nurse practitioner or physician associate. Rules adopted pursuant to this
35 paragraph are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2‑A.
36 SUMMARY
3377 This bill updates the laws governing the licensing and investigation of facilities for
3388 children and adults by clarifying the requirements regarding confidential abuse and neglect
3399 investigation records, requiring the Department of Health and Human Services, instead of
4400 the State Fire Marshal or other public safety inspectors, to inspect specialized children's
4411 homes and removing requirements for interagency licensing of children's homes between
Page 1 - 132LR2800(01)
412 the Department of Health and Human Services and the Department of Education and for
423 licensing of nursery schools. The bill also corrects a cross-reference.
