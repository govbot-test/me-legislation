132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
SECOND REGULAR SESSION-2026
Legislative Document No. 2221
S.P. 905 In Senate, February 24, 2026
Users Relations Advisory Board
Reported by Senator BALDACCI of Penobscot for the Joint Standing Committee on Inland
Fisheries and Wildlife pursuant to Resolve 2025, chapter 82, section 6.
Reference to the Committee on Inland Fisheries and Wildlife suggested and ordered printed
pursuant to Joint Rule 218.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 12 MRSA §10157, sub-§1-A, as amended by PL 2023, c. 405, Pt. A, §28,
3 is further amended to read:
4 1-A. Appointment and composition. The Landowners and Land Users Relations
5 Advisory Board, referred to in this chapter as "the advisory board" and established by Title
6 5, section 12004‑I, subsection 49‑C, consists of the following 18 members:
7 A. Eleven Thirteen voting members, appointed by the Commissioner of Inland
8 Fisheries and Wildlife:
9 (1) One representative of a statewide small woodland owners association;
10 (2) One representative of a large landowners association;
11 (3) One representative of a statewide farmers organization;
12 (4) Three representatives who hunt, fish or trap;
13 (5) Two Three representatives of outdoor recreationists, including one
14 representative of an all-terrain vehicle club and one representative of a
15 nonmotorized recreation industry with a focus on hiking, wildlife watching, biking,
16 paddling, trail running or other popular outdoor pursuits;
17 (6) Two representatives of environmentalist organizations; and
18 (7) One representative of land trust organizations.; and
19 (8) One representative of an organization managing recreational access to the
20 North Maine Woods; and
21 B. Five nonvoting members:
22 (1) Two representatives of the Bureau of Warden Service, designated by the
23 Commissioner of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife;
24 (2) One representative of the Department of Economic and Community
25 Development, designated by the Commissioner of Economic and Community
26 Development; and
27 (3) Two representatives of the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and
28 Forestry, designated by the Commissioner of Agriculture, Conservation and
29 Forestry.
30 SUMMARY
3311 This bill is reported out by the Joint Standing Committee on Inland Fisheries and
3322 Wildlife pursuant to Resolve 2025, chapter 82. The bill amends the composition of the
3333 Landowners and Land Users Relations Advisory Board. It increases the number of outdoor
3344 recreationists on the board and adds specificity about the requisite experience of these
3355 members. It also adds a representative of an organization managing recreational access to
3366 the North Maine Woods. Finally, it adds 5 nonvoting members to the board: two
3377 representatives of the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife, Bureau of Warden
3388 Service, one representative of the Department of Economic and Community Development
3399 and 2 representatives of the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry.
