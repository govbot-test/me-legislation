1 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3 Whereas, the number of protests held on state properties and the need for services
4 provided by state agencies are anticipated to increase in 2026 and coming years; and
5 Whereas, the Department of Public Safety, Bureau of Capitol Police has expressed
6 concern about the lack of specific statutory guidance regarding persons who have been
7 restricted from accessing state properties or services provided by state entities; and
8 Whereas, providing specific and timely statutory guidance on the process of
9 restricting a person from accessing state properties or services provided by state entities or
10 communicating with or through state entities is necessary to address immediate public
11 safety concerns and individual rights; and
12 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
13 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
14 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
15 therefore,
16 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
17 Sec. 1. 5 MRSA §60 is enacted to read:
18 §60. Restriction of access to state property or services; restriction of communication
19 with or through entities of the State
20 1. Entity of the State; definition. As used in this section, "entity of the State" means
21 any office, department, agency, authority, commission, board, institution, hospital or other
22 instrumentality of the State.
23 2. Notice or communication of restriction. An entity of the State that issues a notice
24 or communication that restricts a person from accessing state property or services provided
25 by the State or that restricts a person from communicating with or through the entity shall
26 include in the notice or communication the factual basis for the entity's decision to restrict
27 that person's access or communication, which must be provided to the person. Except as
28 provided in subsection 3, an entity of the State may restrict a person's access to state
29 property or services provided by the State or restrict a person from communicating with or
30 through the entity through a notice or communication for a total period not to exceed 90
31 days.
32 3. Restrictions more than 90 days; protection from harassment. An entity of the
33 State that seeks to extend or renew a restriction issued by notice or communication under
34 subsection 2 for a period exceeding 90 days shall, through the Department of Public Safety,
35 Bureau of State Police, file a complaint for protection from harassment pursuant to chapter
36 337-A and provide evidence to the court of the original notice or communication restricting
37 the person from accessing state property or services provided by the State or restricting the
38 person from communicating with or through the entity, along with the factual basis for the
39 entity's original decision.
40 The court shall prioritize complaints filed for protection from harassment other than those
41 filed pursuant to this subsection.

1 Sec. 2. 5 MRSA §4651, sub-§2, ¶A, as amended by PL 2025, c. 400, §1, is further
2 amended to read:
3 A. Three or more acts of intimidation, confrontation, physical force or the threat of
4 physical force directed against any person, family or, business or, with respect to an
5 action brought under section 60, subsection 3, an entity of the State that are made with
6 the intention of causing fear, intimidation or damage to personal property and that do
7 in fact cause fear, intimidation or damage to personal property;
8 Sec. 3. 5 MRSA §4652, first ¶, as amended by PL 1991, c. 760, §2, is further
9 amended to read:
10 Proceedings under this chapter must be filed, heard and determined in the District Court
11 of the division in which either the plaintiff or the defendant resides. If the plaintiff has left
12 the plaintiff's residence to avoid harassment, the plaintiff may bring an action in the division
13 of the plaintiff's previous residence or new residence. In an action brought under section
14 60, subsection 3 by an entity of the State and filed by the Department of Public Safety,
15 Bureau of State Police, the proceedings may be filed, heard and determined in any division
16 of the plaintiff's choice.
17 Sec. 4. 5 MRSA §4653, sub-§1, as amended by PL 2021, c. 634, Pt. B, §1, is further
18 amended to read:
19 1. Filing. A person who, family, business or, with respect to an action brought under
20 section 60, subsection 3, an entity of the State that has been a victim of harassment,
21 including a business, may seek relief by filing in an appropriate court:
22 A. A sworn complaint alleging harassment; and
23 B. If the alleged harassment does not meet the definition in section 4651, subsection
24 2, paragraph C or is not related to an allegation of domestic violence, violence against
25 a dating partner, sexual assault, stalking or harassment as described in Title 17‑A,
26 section 506, subsection 1, paragraph A‑1 or A‑2, a copy of a notice to stop harassing
27 the plaintiff issued to the defendant pursuant to Title 17‑A, section 506‑A, subsection
28 1, paragraph A, subparagraph (1), division (a) or subparagraph (3) or a statement of
29 good cause why such a notice was not sought or obtained. The court has discretion,
30 based on the nature of the allegations as well as any further inquiry that the court may
31 make of the plaintiff, to issue an order even if notice to stop harassing the plaintiff has
32 not been issued to the defendant as described in Title 17‑A, section 506‑A, subsection
33 1, paragraph A, subparagraph (1), division (a) or subparagraph (3).
34 Sec. 5. 5 MRSA §4653, sub-§4 is enacted to read:
35 4. Entity of the State. An action brought under section 60, subsection 3 by an entity
36 of the State must be filed by the Department of Public Safety, Bureau of State Police on
37 behalf of the entity pursuant to this chapter.
38 Sec. 6. 5 MRSA §4654, sub-§4, ¶F, as amended by PL 2015, c. 410, Pt. C, §2 and
39 c. 443, §2, is further amended by amending subparagraph (2) to read:
40 (2) Being at or in the vicinity of the plaintiff's home, school, business or place of
41 employment or, in the case of an action brought under section 60, subsection 3 by
42 an entity of the State, state property;

1 Sec. 7. 5 MRSA §4655, sub-§1, ¶C-1, as enacted by PL 1993, c. 475, §2, is
2 amended by amending subparagraph (2) to read:
3 (2) Being at or in the vicinity of the plaintiff's home, school, business or place of
4 employment or, in the case of an action brought under section 60, subsection 3 by
5 an entity of the State, state property;
6 Emergency clause. In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation
7 takes effect when approved.
8 SUMMARY
99 This bill prohibits an entity of the State from restricting a person's access to state
1100 property or services provided by the State or restricting a person from communicating with
1111 or through the entity for a period of more than 90 days unless certain conditions are met. It
1122 requires an entity of the State to include on a notice or communication restricting a person's
1133 access or communication the factual basis for the entity's decision. The bill requires an
1144 entity of the State seeking to extend or renew a restriction issued by notice or
1155 communication for more than 90 days to file, through the Department of Public Safety,
1166 Bureau of State Police, a complaint for protection from harassment and provide evidence
1177 to the court of the original notice or communication restricting the person from accessing
1188 state property or services provided by the State or restricting the person from
1199 communicating with or through the entity, along with the factual basis for the entity's
2200 original decision. The bill requires the court to prioritize complaints filed for protection
2211 from harassment that are unrelated to the extension or renewal of a restriction for more than
2222 90 days. The bill clarifies that an action brought by an entity of the State and filed by the
2233 Department of Public Safety, Bureau of State Police may be filed, heard and determined in
2244 any division of the District Court of the plaintiff's choice and that the court may direct a
2255 defendant to refrain from being in the vicinity of state property.
