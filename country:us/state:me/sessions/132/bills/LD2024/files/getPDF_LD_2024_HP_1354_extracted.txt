Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
SECOND REGULAR SESSION-2026
Legislative Document No. 2024
An Act to Make Changes to Certain Licensing Laws Governing For-
hire Charter Boat Operators, Lobster and Crab Fishing and Elver
Dealers
Submitted by the Department of Marine Resources pursuant to Joint Rule 203.
Received by the Clerk of the House on December 3, 2025. Referred to the Committee on
Marine Resources pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed pursuant to Joint Rule 401.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative HEPLER of Woolwich.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 12 MRSA §6313, sub-§4, ¶A, as enacted by PL 2025, c. 155, §5 and
3 affected by §6, is repealed.
4 Sec. 2. 12 MRSA §6313, sub-§4, ¶A-1 is enacted to read:
5 A-1. Is the captain of a vessel licensed to carry passengers for hire for saltwater
6 recreational fishing; or
7 Sec. 3. 12 MRSA §6431-A, sub-§1, as amended by PL 2007, c. 201, §12, is further
8 amended to read:
9 1. Limit. Before March 1, 2000, the holder of a Class I, Class II or Class III license
10 issued under section 6421 may not submerge more than 1,000 traps. After February 29,
11 2000, the The holder of a Class I, Class II or Class III license or a nonresident lobster and
12 crab landing permit issued under section 6421 may not submerge more than 800 traps. If
13 a lower trap limit is adopted by rule for a zone pursuant to section 6446, a license holder
14 who fishes in that zone may not submerge a number of traps that exceeds the lower limit.
15 The number of traps fished from a vessel may not exceed the applicable trap limit
16 established in this subsection, regardless of the number of license holders fishing from that
17 vessel.
18 Sec. 4. 12 MRSA §6431-A, sub-§4, as enacted by PL 2001, c. 421, Pt. B, §22 and
19 affected by Pt. C, §1, is repealed.
20 Sec. 5. 12 MRSA §6864, sub-§1-B, as enacted by PL 2025, c. 370, §3, is repealed.
21 SUMMARY
2222 This bill makes changes to certain licensing laws related to marine resources. It clarifies
2233 eligibility requirements for a for-hire charter boat operator's license. It amends a provision
2244 of law governing lobster and crab trap limits by removing language that describes trap
2255 limits prior to March 1, 2000 and repealing a provision that establishes a specific civil
2266 penalty for violating trap limits. It removes a prohibition on issuing an elver dealer's license
2277 or supplemental license to a person using the same business address as a person who has
2288 violated certain laws governing elver dealers for at least 5 years following the violation.
