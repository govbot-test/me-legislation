1 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3 Whereas, this legislation needs to take effect before the expiration of the 90-day
4 period in order to expand access to vaccines and protect the lives and health of the people
5 of this State; and
6 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
7 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
8 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
9 therefore,
10 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
11 Sec. 1. 22 MRSA §3174-QQQ is enacted to read:
12 §3174-QQQ. Reimbursement for vaccines
13 The department shall provide reimbursement under the MaineCare program for
14 vaccines with no copayments or other out-of-pocket cost sharing, including deductibles,
15 and shall provide reimbursement for off-label use of vaccines. As used in this section, "off-
16 label use" has the same meaning as in Title 24-A, section 4301-A, subsection 11-A.
17 Sec. 2. 24-A MRSA §4301-A, sub-§9-A is enacted to read:
18 9-A. Medically accepted indication. "Medically accepted indication" includes any
19 use of a drug that has been approved by the federal Food and Drug Administration and
20 includes another use of the drug if that use is supported by one or more citations in the
21 standard reference compendia or if the carrier involved, based upon guidance provided by
22 the federal Department of Health and Human Services Medicare program pursuant to 42
23 United States Code, Section 1395x(t), determines that that use is medically accepted based
24 on supportive clinical evidence in peer-reviewed medical literature.
25 Sec. 3. 24-A MRSA §4301-A, sub-§11-A is enacted to read:
26 11-A. Off-label use. "Off-label use" means the prescription and use of drugs for
27 medically accepted indications other than those stated in the labeling approved by the
28 federal Food and Drug Administration.
29 Sec. 4. 24-A MRSA §4320-W is enacted to read:
30 §4320-W. Coverage for vaccines
31 A carrier offering a health plan in this State shall provide coverage for vaccines without
32 any deductible, copayment, coinsurance or other cost-sharing requirement and shall
33 provide coverage for off-label use of vaccines.
34 Sec. 5. 32 MRSA §13831, sub-§1, as amended by PL 2023, c. 170, §1, is further
35 amended to read:
36 1. Administration of influenza vaccines. A pharmacist licensed in this State who
37 meets the qualifications and requirements of section 13832 and rules adopted by the board
38 may prescribe or dispense or administer topically or by injection or by inhalation all forms
39 of influenza vaccines, including intranasal influenza vaccines, to a person 3 years of age or
40 older without a prescription.

30 Emergency clause. In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation
31 takes effect when approved.
32 SUMMARY
3333 This bill allows pharmacists to prescribe, dispense or administer vaccines. It requires
3344 insurance carriers and the MaineCare program to provide coverage for vaccines without
3355 any deductible, copayment, coinsurance or other cost-sharing reimbursement and to
3366 provide coverage for off-label use of vaccines.
